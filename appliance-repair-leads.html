Frequently Asked Questions

How much do appliance repair leads cost?
Lead costs vary by platform and location. Google Ads typically ranges $30–$70 per lead, while Facebook Ads ranges $25–$60. Emergency repairs command higher costs than maintenance/preventative calls. Your actual cost depends on competition in your market and campaign optimization.
Which is better — Google Ads or Facebook Ads?
Both serve different purposes. Google Ads captures people actively searching for "refrigerator repair near me" (high-intent, expensive). Facebook Ads builds brand awareness and retargets people who've visited your site (lower cost per lead but requires more volume). Most repair businesses use both for maximum reach.
How long does it take to get appliance repair leads?
Google Ads can generate leads within 24–48 hours if campaigns are set up correctly. Facebook Ads typically needs 1–2 weeks of data to optimize targeting. SEO is a long-term play, taking 3–6 months to see meaningful results. Most repairs businesses see their first qualified lead within the first week of running ads.
What's the best budget to start with?
Start with $500–$1,500 per month to test strategies and gather data. This allows you to run Google Ads ($20–$30/day) AND Facebook Ads ($10–$20/day) simultaneously. Track your cost per lead and adjust. Once you find profitable campaigns, scale the budget 10–20% at a time.
Can I get appliance repair leads without paid ads?
Yes, through SEO and local search optimization. Claim and optimize your Google Business Profile, build local citations, and create content around common repair keywords. However, SEO takes 3–6 months. Paid ads generate immediate leads while SEO builds long-term organic traffic.
How do I qualify appliance repair leads?
Ask qualifying questions on your landing page or during the call: What appliance needs repair? Is it under warranty? Does the customer have emergency availability? What's their budget? Leads for emergency repairs are higher quality. Use call tracking to know which ads source the best-qualified calls.
