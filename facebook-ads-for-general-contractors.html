Facebook Ads for General Contractors: How to Consistently Land High-Ticket Jobs

Stop relying on referrals alone. Learn how to generate consistent, high-ticket contractor jobs using Facebook Ads.

Most general contractors rely heavily on referrals, word-of-mouth, and platforms like HomeStars or Houzz. While those can work, they're inconsistent and hard to scale.

Facebook Ads solve that problem by giving you a predictable way to generate homeowner leads every single month.

In this guide, you'll learn exactly how to use Facebook Ads for general contractors to land high-ticket jobs consistently.

Why Facebook Ads Work So Well for General Contractors

General contracting is one of the highest ROI niches for Facebook Ads because:

  • Jobs are high-ticket ($5,000–$100,000+)
  • Homeowners respond strongly to visual transformations
  • You only need a few jobs per month to win big

Unlike Google Ads (which captures people actively searching), Facebook allows you to get in front of homeowners before they even start looking. You're creating demand, not just reacting to it.

The Best Offers for Contractor Ads

Your offer determines whether your campaign wins or loses. A weak offer = no leads.

Top-performing offers include:

  • Free in-home estimate
  • $1,000+ off renovation projects
  • Free design consultation
  • "Book this month, save X%"

The key is to remove friction and give homeowners a reason to act now. Vague offers get ignored.

Creative That Converts (This Is Non-Negotiable)

Contractors win with proof. Your creative must showcase real work.

Use these proven formats:

  • Before and After Remodels – Kitchen/bathroom transformations work best
  • Walkthrough Videos – Show finished jobs in full detail
  • Time-Lapse Renovations – Fast, compelling proof of work
  • Project Portfolio Slides – Multiple jobs in one carousel

Bad creatives = no leads.
This is non-negotiable. Invest in quality before/afters or you're wasting money.

Targeting Strategy (Keep It Simple)

You don't need complex targeting. Broad local targeting works best for contractors:

  • Radius: 15–30 km around your service area
  • Age: 30–65+
  • Homeowners: Target homeowner status if available

Pro Tip: Broad targeting often beats narrow, complex audiences. Let Facebook's algorithm find qualified homeowners.

Campaign Setup (Simple & Effective)

Campaign Objective:

  • Leads (using Instant Forms)

Starting Budget:

  • $20–$60/day to start

Campaign Structure:

  • 1 campaign
  • 1–2 ad sets
  • 2–3 creatives minimum (different jobs, angles)

Avoid overcomplication. This simple structure converts extremely well.

Lead Form Setup (Keep It Short)

Shorter forms = more leads. Keep it to the essentials:

  • Name
  • Phone number
  • Project type (renovation, remodel, etc.)
  • Timeline for project

Optional: Budget range (helps you qualify leads before calling).

Follow-Up System (Speed Wins Jobs)

Speed is everything in contracting. Homeowners get multiple quotes. The fastest contractor wins.

Best practices:

  • Call within 5 minutes – Don't wait
  • Pre-qualify budget – Understand their scope
  • Book on-site estimate ASAP – In-person meetings close jobs

Example message: "Hey! Saw you requested a free estimate. We've got availability this week—what day works best for a quick walkthrough?"

Expected Results (What to Expect)

Typical performance for general contractors:

  • Cost per lead: $15–$50
  • Leads per month: 10–40 (depending on budget)
  • Job conversion: 1–2 jobs typically covers months of ad spend

One $10,000 kitchen renovation job pays for months of ads. The ROI is incredible if you follow up properly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Weak offer
"Contact us" doesn't work. Give homeowners a reason to respond.

Poor quality visuals
Bad before/afters kill campaigns. Use high-quality project photos.

Slow response time
If you don't call in 5 minutes, someone else will. Speed matters.

No sales process
Ads bring leads, but your team closes jobs. Train them to sell.

Final Thoughts

Facebook Ads give contractors control over their pipeline. No more hoping for referrals.

The winning formula:

  • Strong, specific offer
  • High-quality proof (before/afters)
  • Simple, local targeting
  • Fast follow-up (under 5 minutes)

Execute that consistently and you'll never rely on referrals again.
