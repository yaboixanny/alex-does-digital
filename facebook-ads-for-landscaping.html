Facebook Ads for Landscaping: How to Get Consistent Local Leads

If you're relying on referrals and seasonal spikes, you're leaving money on the table. Learn exactly how to use Facebook Ads to generate consistent, high-quality landscaping leads on demand.

← Back to Blog

If you're running a landscaping business and relying on referrals or seasonal spikes, you're leaving a lot of money on the table.

Facebook Ads give you a predictable way to generate landscaping leads on demand — whether you do lawn care, hardscaping, or full backyard transformations.

In this guide, I'll break down exactly how to use Facebook Ads to get consistent, high-quality landscaping leads.

Why Facebook Ads Work So Well for Landscaping

Landscaping is a visual and local service — which is exactly what Facebook and Instagram are built for.

People scroll these platforms daily, and when they see a clean yard transformation or a beautiful patio install, it grabs attention immediately.

Here's why it works:

  • Highly targeted local reach (zip code or radius targeting)
  • Strong visual appeal (before/after transformations)
  • Lower cost per lead compared to Google in many markets
  • Ability to capture demand before people search

The Best Offer for Landscaping Ads

Most landscaping ads fail because the offer is weak.

"Contact us for a quote" is not compelling.

Instead, you want a clear, simple offer that gives people a reason to act now.

High-performing landscaping offers:

  • Free estimate (with fast turnaround)
  • "Spring yard cleanup special"
  • "$500 off patio installations over $5,000"
  • "Same-week lawn care availability"
  • Seasonal promos (fall cleanup, summer prep, etc.)

Make it specific, timely, and easy to understand.

The Best Ad Creative (What Actually Gets Clicks)

Your creative is everything.

The goal is to stop the scroll and instantly show the result.

What works best:

1. Before & After Photos

Messy yard → clean, professional result. Huge attention grabber.

2. Short Video Walkthroughs

Show the finished project. Add quick voiceover or captions.

3. Real Job Footage

Crews working. Equipment in action. Builds trust instantly.

Example Ad Copy

Primary Text:

Tired of looking at your yard every day and knowing it needs work?

We help homeowners in [City] transform their outdoor space without the stress.

Right now, we're offering FREE estimates and fast scheduling for landscaping projects.

Tap below to get a quote.

Headline:

Get a Free Landscaping Quote

Targeting Setup (Keep This Simple)

You don't need complicated targeting.

Start with:

  • Location: 10–25 mile radius around your service area
  • Age: 30–65+ (homeowners typically)
  • Interests: Leave broad OR test home improvement / gardening

Pro tip: Broad targeting often works best with good creative.

Lead Form vs Landing Page

For most landscaping businesses, Facebook Lead Forms win — especially if budget is under ~$50/day.

Lead Forms:

  • Faster
  • Higher conversion rates
  • Lower cost per lead

Landing Pages:

  • Higher quality leads (sometimes)
  • Better for bigger projects ($5k+ jobs)

If you're just starting — use Lead Forms.

Budget & Expected Results

A realistic starting point:

  • Budget: $20–$50/day
  • Cost per lead: $10–$40 (varies by market)
  • Leads per month: 30–100+

The key is consistency and follow-up.

The #1 Mistake Landscaping Businesses Make

Slow follow-up.

If you don't call or text leads quickly, you lose them.

Best practice:

  • Call within 5–10 minutes
  • Follow up multiple times
  • Have a simple script ready

Speed = revenue.

Simple Campaign Setup

  • Campaign Objective: Leads
  • Use Instant Forms
  • 1 campaign – 1 ad set – 2–3 ads
  • Test different creatives (before/after, video, etc.)

Keep it simple and optimize based on results.

Final Thoughts

Facebook Ads are one of the fastest ways to grow a landscaping business if you do them right.

You don't need a massive budget — you just need:

  • Strong visuals
  • A clear offer
  • Fast follow-up

Dial those in, and you can generate consistent landscaping leads every month.
