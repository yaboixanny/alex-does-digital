How to Be 100% Sure Google Ads Will Work (Before You Sign Anything)

Never lock yourself into a long-term contract until you understand exactly what you're getting. Learn how a pilot campaign lets you test Google Ads risk-free.

← Back to Blog

If you're thinking about running Google Ads, here's the truth:

You should never lock yourself into a long-term contract until you understand exactly what you're getting.

Before I run ads for any client, I make sure we know three things:

  • How many leads you're likely to get each month
  • How much those leads will cost
  • How much you should realistically budget for ad spend

If you don't know those three numbers, you're guessing—and that's where people lose money.

Why Most Google Ads Setups Fail

Google can't tell you if Google Ads will work for your business.

And a lot of agencies will still try to push you into:

  • 3–6 month contracts
  • High retainers
  • No real clarity on results

That's not a strategy—that's a gamble.

So instead of doing that, I run what I call a pilot campaign.

The Pilot Campaign (Low-Risk Testing)

A pilot campaign is a short-term test—usually 30 to 60 days—designed to answer one question:

Will Google Ads actually work for your business?

No long-term commitment. No guessing.

Just real data.

What You'll Know After the Pilot

By the end of the pilot, you'll have clear answers:

  • Cost per lead
  • Estimated monthly lead volume
  • Lead quality (are these actually good prospects?)

At that point, you can make a decision based on data—not opinions.

What I Actually Build During the Pilot

This isn't just "turn ads on and hope."

Here's what goes into it:

  • Keyword research to find exactly what people are searching when they need your service
  • High-converting Google Ads built around those searches
  • A proper landing page (not your homepage) designed to convert traffic into calls + form submissions
  • Lead tracking so every call and form is captured
  • Campaign monitoring and daily optimization
  • Data analysis so we understand what's working and what's not

Everything is built around one goal: generating real, trackable leads.

Cost of the Pilot

There are two parts to this:

1. Setup + Management

$2,000 one-time

This covers:

  • Full campaign build
  • Landing page creation
  • Tracking setup
  • Campaign management during the pilot
  • Ongoing optimization

2. Ad Spend (Paid to Google)

This is separate.

Google charges you per click, and the exact cost depends on your market.

I'll recommend a starting budget—but you control it 100%.

You'll never spend more than what you're comfortable with.

How Long Does It Take?

Most of the time, we can get clear answers in about 30 days.

In smaller markets, it might take a bit longer to gather enough data.

But the goal is always the same: get enough data to make a confident decision.

Will You Get Sales During the Pilot?

Sometimes yes—but that's not the main goal.

The pilot is designed to test: lead generation, not closing.

Here's why that matters:

  • Some leads take time to convert (especially higher-ticket services)
  • If you're not used to handling inbound leads, there can be a learning curve
  • Sales performance depends on your follow-up, not just the ads

Two important scenarios:

1. Leads convert, but not profitably

Ads are working, but numbers don't make sense.

2. Leads don't convert at all

Likely a sales/process issue, not an ads issue.

That's why we separate: lead quality vs. sales performance.

What Happens After the Pilot?

At the end, you'll have a clear answer.

If Google Ads don't work:

You just saved yourself months of wasted budget and frustration.

If Google Ads do work:

You now have:

  • A proven lead source
  • Real performance data
  • A live campaign ready to scale

No Lock-In, No Pressure

If you want to continue, great—I'll help you scale it.

If not, no problem.

You still walk away with:

  • Data
  • Insights
  • A working campaign

Final Take

Most people lose money on Google Ads because they commit too early.

The smarter move is simple:

Test first → Validate with data → Then scale

That's how you remove risk—and actually make Google Ads work.
